# Napping Strategy

How to nap during jetlag recovery without sabotaging night sleep.

## Keep naps short

A recovery nap should be short — roughly 20 to 30 minutes — and timed to restore alertness
without dropping you into deep sleep. Kept brief, it tops up energy while leaving the night's
sleep largely intact. This is the only kind of nap that reliably helps during jetlag.

## Why long naps backfire

A nap of an hour or more lets you fall into deep sleep and burns off the sleep pressure you
need that night. You may then lie awake at 2 a.m., setting your adjustment back. The longer
and later the nap, the more it works against you.

## When not to nap

If you arrive in the evening or are close to local bedtime, skip the nap and go to bed on the
local schedule. The goal is always to protect that first solid night of local-time sleep,
which does more for adjustment than any daytime nap.
