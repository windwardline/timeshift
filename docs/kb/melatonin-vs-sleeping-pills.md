# Melatonin vs. Sleeping Pills

Two very different ways to deal with sleeplessness on a trip.

## Different jobs

Melatonin is a timing signal that shifts when your body expects night; it gently encourages
sleep at the new hour. A sedative or hypnotic sleeping pill forces sleep but does nothing to
move the clock, so it treats the symptom without addressing the underlying mismatch.

## When a pill might fit

A clinician may occasionally prescribe a short course of a sleep medication to secure rest at
the destination or avoid severe sleep loss in the first days. It is not a routine jetlag
treatment and carries grogginess and dependence concerns that melatonin does not.

## The grounded approach

For most travelers, shifting the clock with light, schedule, and modest melatonin beats
masking the problem with sedatives. If sleep is badly disrupted and important performance is
at stake, discuss options with a doctor rather than self-medicating.
