# Crossing the Date Line

Why the calendar flip looks confusing but does not change the plan.

## The date changes, the logic doesn't

Crossing the International Date Line changes the calendar date but not the underlying jetlag
logic. What matters for your clock is the number of time zones and the direction of travel,
not which calendar day it happens to be when you land.

## Focus on zones and direction

You can leave on one day and arrive on what looks like a different day, which feels strange,
but the real adjustment still depends on how many zones you crossed and whether you went east
or west. Work the same "east = early, west = late" plan you would anywhere.

## Don't overthink the date

Let the app or your itinerary handle the calendar; you focus on light, sleep, and meal timing
for the zone shift. The date-line crossing is a bookkeeping quirk, not an extra dose of
jetlag.
