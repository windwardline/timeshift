# Light Timing for Eastward Trips

How to use light to advance your clock after flying east.

## Seek morning light

Heading east you need to advance your clock, and morning light at the destination is the main
tool. Get outside in the hours after your local wake time so the bright signal pulls your
clock earlier. A morning walk is an ideal way to deliver it.

## Avoid late-evening light

Bright light late in the evening delays the clock — the opposite of what an eastward traveler
wants. In the first days, ease off screens and bright indoor light before bed, and consider
dim surroundings as local bedtime approaches.

## Watch the early-morning trap

Very early in adjustment, light received before your body's temperature minimum can still
delay you. If you wake extremely early local time, hold off on bright light for a short while,
then seek it once true local morning arrives.
