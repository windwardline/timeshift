# Zeitgebers: The Time Cues

The external signals that reset your clock, ranked by strength.

## What a zeitgeber is

A zeitgeber — German for "time-giver" — is any external cue that helps set the body clock.
The clock is not sealed off; it constantly recalibrates to these signals. After crossing
time zones, the destination's zeitgebers are what eventually pull your clock into line.

## The hierarchy of cues

Light is the dominant zeitgeber by a wide margin. Below it sit meal timing, physical
activity, and social or work schedules, which reinforce the shift but cannot override badly
timed light. The practical lesson is to get light right first, then stack the others in the
same direction.

## Using them together

The fastest adjustment comes from aligning several cues at once: morning light, breakfast,
and activity together signal "morning" far more strongly than any one alone. Conflicting
cues — bright light at the wrong hour, or meals on home time — slow you down.
