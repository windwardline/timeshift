# Exercise and Adjustment

How physical activity can support — or sabotage — a clock shift.

## Activity as a cue

Physical activity is a secondary time cue that can reinforce a clock shift, and getting moving
also fights the daytime grogginess of jetlag. An outdoor walk is especially useful because it
combines exercise with bright light.

## Time it with the day

Daytime activity at the destination helps you stay awake and signals "day" to the clock.
Vigorous exercise too close to local bedtime, however, can be stimulating and delay sleep, so
keep hard workouts away from the evening when you are trying to sleep earlier.

## Keep it gentle at first

In the first day or two, fatigue and dehydration make heavy training a poor idea. Light to
moderate activity, timed for the local day and paired with daylight, supports adjustment
without adding strain.
