# Melatonin Dosing

Why less is often more when it comes to melatonin.

## Low doses work

For shifting the clock, a small dose is typically as effective as a large one, because the
goal is a timing signal rather than sedation. Research on jetlag has found low doses to be
effective, and larger amounts mainly add grogginess rather than benefit.

## Why bigger is not better

High doses can leave melatonin circulating into the next day, causing daytime drowsiness, and
they do not shift the clock any more reliably. They can also raise the odds of side effects
without improving the result you actually want.

## Quality and consistency

Because melatonin is sold as a supplement, the actual content of a product can vary from its
label. Choosing a reputable product and keeping the dose modest and consistent gives the most
predictable timing effect. When in doubt, start low.
