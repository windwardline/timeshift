# In-Flight Strategy

What to do in the air to arrive closer to destination time.

## Set your watch to destination time

A simple but powerful first move is to set your watch — and your mindset — to destination
time as you board. Deciding it is now "night in Tokyo" or "morning in London" frames every
in-flight choice about when to sleep, eat, and seek light. The mental switch helps you
start living on the new schedule before you land.

## When to sleep versus stay awake

Once you are on destination time, sleep on the plane during the hours that are night at
your destination and try to stay awake when it is daytime there. On an eastward red-eye
that means sleeping for the destination's night even if it is still evening back home.
Aligning in-air sleep to destination night is the heart of the in-flight plan.

## Light and eye-mask use aloft

You can manage light in the cabin to support your plan: use an eye mask and dim your
screen when it is destination night, and open the window or keep the cabin light when it is
destination day. Blocking light during your target sleep window protects it, while letting
light in during destination daytime helps you stay awake. The same seek-or-avoid logic from
the ground applies in the air.

## Movement, hydration, and meals

Get up and move periodically to keep circulation going on a long flight, and keep drinking
water to counter the dry cabin air. Try to eat in line with destination mealtimes rather
than whenever trays arrive, since meal timing is another clock cue. Going easy on alcohol
aloft keeps your eventual sleep from fragmenting.
