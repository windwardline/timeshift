# Phase Advance vs. Phase Delay

The two ways a clock can move, and which direction needs which.

## Advancing the clock

A phase advance shifts your clock earlier, so you feel sleepy and wake earlier. Eastward
travel requires this. It is the harder direction because the body resists shortening its day,
and it relies on morning light, earlier melatonin, and an earlier bedtime.

## Delaying the clock

A phase delay shifts your clock later, so you stay up and wake later. Westward travel
requires this, and it aligns with the clock's natural tendency to run long. Evening light, a
later bedtime, and sleeping in drive the delay.

## Matching tools to direction

Every adjustment tool — light, melatonin, meals, naps — can push the clock either way
depending on when you apply it. The skill is knowing whether your trip needs an advance or a
delay, then timing each tool to push in that single direction rather than against itself.
