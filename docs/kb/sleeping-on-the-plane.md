# Sleeping on the Plane

When to sleep aloft and how to make it count.

## Sleep on destination night

Whether to sleep in the air depends on the clock at your destination, not the one you left.
If it is night where you are going, sleeping on the plane helps you arrive closer to local
time; if it is daytime there, staying awake is usually better.

## Protect the window

When you do want to sleep aloft, protect the window: an eye mask and earplugs or headphones
block light and noise, a neck pillow helps, and skipping the late meal and alcohol keeps sleep
from fragmenting. Recline when you can and dim your screen.

## Do not force it

If you cannot sleep when destination night says you should, rest quietly rather than fighting
it, and avoid bright screens that would push your clock the wrong way. Even relaxed wakeful
rest beats stimulating yourself out of the target window.
