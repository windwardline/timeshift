# Hydration on Long-Haul Flights

Why staying hydrated reduces avoidable jetlag-like misery.

## Dry cabin air

Aircraft cabin air is very dry, and long flights make dehydration easy. Dehydration amplifies
fatigue, headaches, and grogginess — symptoms that feel like jetlag and stack on top of the
real clock mismatch. Drinking water steadily through the flight blunts them.

## What to limit

Both alcohol and excess caffeine aloft worsen dehydration and disrupt sleep, so go easy on
them in the air. Plain water and staying comfortable do more for how you feel on arrival than
any in-flight drink.

## Hydration is not a clock fix

Drinking water will not move your body clock, but it removes a layer of avoidable symptoms so
that your actual adjustment work — light, sleep, and meal timing — is not buried under simple
dehydration.
