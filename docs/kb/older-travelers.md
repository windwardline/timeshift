# Older Travelers

Why adjustment can be slower with age, and how to adapt the plan.

## Slower recovery

Adjustment tends to be slower for older travelers, and recovering from lost sleep gets harder
with age. A more gradual, gentler approach usually works better than aggressive tactics that
assume a young, flexible clock.

## Gentler tactics

Lean on consistent routines: steady sleep and wake times, regular meals, and well-timed
daylight, introduced gradually. Avoid sudden, large schedule jumps, and be patient — the same
tools work, just over more days.

## Medication and timing

Older adults may also be more sensitive to melatonin, which can linger and cause daytime
drowsiness, and may have fixed medication schedules to weave into the plan. When in doubt,
check timing of supplements and medicines with a clinician.
