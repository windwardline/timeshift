# Red-Eye Flights

How to handle an overnight flight that lands in the morning.

## The hard arrival

A red-eye that lands in the morning drops you into a full local day when your body expects
night — one of the tougher arrival patterns. You step off tired, having slept little, and need
to function while your clock insists it is the middle of the night.

## Sleep in the air

Make the most of the destination's night hours aloft: protect a sleep window with an eye mask
and earplugs, skip the late meal and alcohol, and recline when you can. Even a few hours of
real sleep eases the first day.

## Survive the first day

On arrival, get outdoor light, eat on local time, and push through to local bedtime with only
short naps if needed. Plan a light first day and protect that first night, after which things
improve quickly.
