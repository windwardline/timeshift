# Circadian Basics

What the body clock is and why it governs how you feel across the day.

## What the body clock is

Your body runs on an internal clock that keeps a roughly 24-hour rhythm even with no
outside cues. This rhythm governs when you feel sleepy, when you feel alert, your core body
temperature, and the release of hormones like melatonin and cortisol. Left in constant
darkness the human clock tends to run a little longer than 24 hours, which is why daily
cues are needed to keep it anchored to the real day.

## Why the rhythm matters for travel

Because the clock controls so many systems at once, moving quickly across time zones throws
all of them out of step with local time at the same moment. That coordinated mismatch is
what makes jetlag feel like more than simple tiredness. Understanding the clock is the first
step to nudging it deliberately rather than fighting it.

## The cues that set it

The clock is reset mainly by light, and secondarily by meal timing, activity, and social
schedules. These cues are called zeitgebers, or "time-givers." Using them on purpose — at
the right times for your direction of travel — is how you speed adjustment.
