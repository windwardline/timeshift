# Strategic Naps vs. Crash Naps

The difference between a nap that helps and one that resets your progress.

## The strategic nap

A strategic nap is short and deliberate — around 20 to 30 minutes, taken early in the local
afternoon. It eases daytime fatigue enough to get you to local bedtime while leaving plenty
of sleep pressure for the night. Set an alarm so it stays brief.

## The crash nap

The crash nap is the trap: lying down exhausted in the late afternoon and sleeping two or
three hours. It bleeds off the sleep pressure you need for the night and can leave you wide
awake after midnight, undoing a day of progress.

## Choosing well

If you must rest, keep it short and early. When you can, push through to local bedtime instead
— a solid first night does more for your clock than any long afternoon sleep.
