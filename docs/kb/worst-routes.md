# Which Routes Hit Hardest

The trips most likely to wreck your clock, and why.

## Many zones, heading east

The hardest routes combine a large number of time zones with an eastward direction — North
America to Europe, or Europe to East Asia. Eastward forces the difficult phase advance, and
more zones mean a bigger shift, so the two effects compound.

## Morning-arrival red-eyes

An overnight flight that lands in the morning is especially brutal, because you step into a
full local day when your body expects night. You arrive needing to function while your clock
insists it is the middle of the night, on top of a short, broken sleep aloft.

## The gentler cases

Westward trips of the same distance tend to be more forgiving, and trips of three or fewer
zones rarely cause true jetlag. Knowing a route is a hard one is a cue to pre-adjust before
departure and to plan a light first day on arrival.
