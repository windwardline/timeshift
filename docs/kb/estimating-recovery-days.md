# Estimating Recovery Days

A rough way to predict how long adjustment will take.

## A day per zone, roughly

A common estimate for eastward travel is about one day of recovery per time zone crossed.
Westward is often a bit faster, closer to two-thirds of a day per zone. So six zones east
might take around six days to settle fully, while the same trip west might take four.

## What changes the estimate

These are averages. Disciplined light, sleep, and meal timing can shorten recovery, while
poor sleep en route, alcohol, and ignoring local cues can lengthen it. Individual differences
matter too — some people adapt faster than others.

## Planning around it

Use the estimate to set expectations, not to promise a date. If you have something important
soon after landing, assume you will only be partly adjusted and schedule demanding tasks for
hours when your body will be reasonably alert.
