# Seeking vs. Avoiding Light

Treating light avoidance as an active tool, not just an afterthought.

## Both directions are tools

Most advice focuses on getting light, but deliberately blocking it is equally powerful. To
shift the clock cleanly you both seek light at the helpful time and avoid it at the harmful
time, so the two reinforce a single direction of movement.

## A quick rule by direction

Eastward: seek morning light, avoid late-evening light. Westward: seek evening light, avoid
early-morning light. Keeping the "seek" and "avoid" windows straight prevents you from
accidentally cancelling your own progress.

## Practical ways to block light

Sunglasses outdoors, dim indoor lighting, an eye mask, and blackout curtains all let you
withhold light when you want to. Used on purpose at the right hour, blocking light is as
useful as chasing it.
