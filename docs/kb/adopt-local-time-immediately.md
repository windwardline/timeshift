# Adopt Local Time Immediately

Why switching to the destination schedule at once speeds adjustment.

## One clock, not two

Trying to keep one foot on home time and one on local time gives your body conflicting cues
and prolongs jetlag. Adopting the destination's schedule for sleep, meals, and activity right
away gives the clock a single, consistent target to move toward.

## Eat and sleep local

Take meals at the destination's normal mealtimes and aim to sleep at the local bedtime, even
if you are only mildly tired. Eating and sleeping on the new schedule are strong reinforcing
cues; resist a full meal at 3 a.m. local just because your stomach is on home time.

## Let light lead

Pair the new schedule with correctly timed light and the shift accelerates. The mental switch
to "it is now this hour here" frames every choice about when to seek light, when to eat, and
when to rest.
