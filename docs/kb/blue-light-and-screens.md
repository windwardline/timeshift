# Blue Light and Screens

Why phones and tablets can quietly delay your adjustment.

## Screens suppress melatonin

Phones, tablets, computers, and TVs emit blue-rich light that suppresses melatonin and
signals "daytime" to the clock. Used late at night, they delay your clock and make it harder
to fall asleep on local time — a particular problem for eastward travelers trying to sleep
earlier.

## When screens hurt and help

For an eastward trip, dim or put away screens in the hours before local bedtime so melatonin
can rise on time. For a westward trip, evening screen light is less of a problem and can even
help you stay up to the later bedtime. Match screen use to your direction.

## Practical steps

Lowering brightness, using night modes, and stopping screen use before bed all reduce the
delaying effect. When you genuinely need light to stay awake, bright room or outdoor light is
a better, stronger signal than a small screen.
