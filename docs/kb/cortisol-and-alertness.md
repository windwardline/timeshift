# Cortisol and Morning Alertness

The hormone that helps wake you up, and how travel disrupts it.

## The morning cortisol rise

Cortisol naturally surges in the early morning to help you wake and feel alert, then tapers
across the day. This rhythm is set by the same master clock that governs melatonin, and the
two work in opposition — cortisol up for day, melatonin up for night.

## How jetlag scrambles it

After crossing zones, your cortisol peak is still tied to home morning, so you may feel a jolt
of alertness in the middle of the destination night and a slump during its day. This is one
reason jetlag fatigue comes in waves rather than as steady tiredness.

## Realigning it

You cannot dose cortisol like melatonin, but you can shift its rhythm with the same tools:
morning light, activity, and a consistent wake time on local schedule. As the clock moves,
the cortisol rise migrates to the local morning where it belongs.
