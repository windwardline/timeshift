# What Jet Lag Actually Is

A clear definition of jetlag and what separates it from ordinary tiredness.

## A clock-versus-clock mismatch

Jetlag is the temporary mismatch between your internal clock and local time after you cross
several time zones quickly. Your body still runs on home time while the destination keeps a
different schedule, so you feel wide awake at the wrong hours and exhausted at others. It is
a desynchronization, not simply a lack of sleep.

## How long it lasts

Severity and duration depend on how many time zones you crossed, the direction of travel,
how well you sleep en route, and how consistently you take the destination's time cues.
Symptoms typically ease over several days as the clock catches up. The further you travel,
especially eastward, the longer recovery tends to take.

## Who gets it most

Frequent flyers, flight crews, and athletes who travel for competition deal with jetlag
most often. Crossing three or fewer zones usually produces travel fatigue rather than true
jetlag, and that fades within a day or two.
