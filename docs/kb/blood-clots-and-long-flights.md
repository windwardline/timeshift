# Blood Clots and Long Flights

A health risk to know about on the same long flights that cause jetlag.

## The basic risk

Air travel longer than about four hours can modestly raise the risk of blood clots, including
deep vein thrombosis, because prolonged sitting lets blood pool in the legs. The longer the
flight and the more immobile you are, the more the risk applies.

## Reducing it

Move your legs frequently, walk the aisle when you can, exercise your calves while seated,
avoid tight clothing, and stay hydrated. These are the same habits that also reduce stiffness
and travel fatigue.

## When to seek advice

Signs like swelling, pain, or warmth in one leg, or shortness of breath after a long flight,
warrant prompt medical attention. Travelers with known risk factors should talk to a clinician
about prevention before flying.
