# Jet Lag Symptoms

The common signs of jetlag and why each one happens.

## Sleep and alertness

The hallmark symptoms are disturbed sleep at night and sleepiness or fatigue during the day.
Because your clock still expects night when it is day at the destination, you feel drowsy at
the wrong times and struggle to sleep when you should. Broken, shallow sleep is typical for
the first nights.

## Mind and mood

Many travelers notice difficulty concentrating, slowed thinking, and a general fog, along
with irritability or low mood. These follow from both the clock mismatch and the sleep loss
that comes with it. Performance on demanding tasks can dip for a few days.

## Body and digestion

Jetlag often upsets the stomach, because the gut has its own clock that is still on home
time and expecting meals at different hours. Headaches and a general feeling of malaise are
common too. Staying hydrated and adopting local mealtimes helps these settle.
