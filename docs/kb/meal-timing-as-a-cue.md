# Meal Timing as a Cue

Why when you eat helps set your clock, not just what you eat.

## The gut has a clock

Your digestive system runs on its own clock that expects food at certain hours. Eating on the
destination's schedule sends a "this is the local day" signal that helps pull your rhythm into
line, while eating on home time keeps the gut — and your digestion — out of step.

## Anchor to local mealtimes

Take meals at the destination's normal breakfast, lunch, and dinner times from arrival day,
even if your appetite disagrees at first. A consistent local eating pattern reinforces the
light and sleep cues working in the same direction.

## Keep it sensible

Heavy, calorie-rich meals late at night can disrupt sleep, so favor lighter evening meals as
you adjust. The main lever is timing — regular meals on local hours — rather than any special
diet.
