# Core Body Temperature Rhythm

How your temperature cycle marks the low point of your clock.

## The daily temperature curve

Core body temperature rises and falls on a daily cycle, reaching its lowest point in the
early hours of the morning, a couple of hours before your usual wake time. Sleepiness is
strongest around this temperature minimum, and alertness climbs as temperature rises through
the day.

## Why it matters for travel

The temperature minimum is a useful landmark because light given just after it tends to
advance the clock, while light given just before it tends to delay the clock. Knowing
roughly where your minimum sits — and that it moves with you across zones — helps you time
light correctly for your direction.

## A moving target

After a flight, your temperature rhythm is still on home time and shifts only gradually. As
it migrates toward local time over several days, your worst grogginess will drift to a more
normal hour. Tracking how you feel can hint at where your rhythm currently sits.
