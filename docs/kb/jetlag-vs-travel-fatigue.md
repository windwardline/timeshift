# Jet Lag vs. Travel Fatigue

Why not all post-flight tiredness is jetlag — and why it matters.

## Two different problems

Travel fatigue is the general exhaustion of a long journey: cramped seating, dehydration,
disrupted routine, and broken sleep. It can happen even on a north–south flight that crosses
no time zones. Jetlag is specifically the clock-versus-clock mismatch from crossing zones,
and it persists until your internal clock catches up.

## Telling them apart

If you crossed three or fewer time zones, your tiredness is most likely travel fatigue and
should fade within a day or two of rest and hydration. If you crossed many zones, especially
eastward, and feel wide awake or exhausted at odd local hours for several days, that is
jetlag. The distinction guides whether you simply rest or actively shift your clock.

## Why it matters

Travel fatigue responds to rest, fluids, and a normal night's sleep. Jetlag needs the clock
to move, which takes deliberate light, meal, and sleep timing. Treating one as the other
wastes effort.
