# Caffeine's Long Half-Life

Why an afternoon coffee can still be in your system at bedtime.

## Hours, not minutes

Caffeine has a long half-life — often around five to six hours — meaning a meaningful amount
remains in your body hours after you drink it. A late-afternoon coffee can still be active
when you try to sleep, fragmenting rest even if you fall asleep at first.

## Setting a cut-off

Because of that slow clearance, set a caffeine cut-off in the early afternoon on local time,
roughly six to eight hours before your target bedtime. An espresso after dinner can quietly
undermine the very night you are trying to build.

## Individual differences

People clear caffeine at different rates, and sensitivity rises with age and with poor sleep.
If you are sensitive or already sleep-deprived from travel, err toward an earlier cut-off and
a smaller dose.
