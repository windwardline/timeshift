# Light Timing for Westward Trips

How to use light to delay your clock after flying west.

## Seek evening light

Heading west you need to delay your clock, and light in the late afternoon and evening is the
main tool. Staying in bright conditions into the early evening at the destination pushes your
clock later so you can stay up to the local bedtime.

## Go easy on early light

Bright light very early in the morning advances the clock, which works against a westward
traveler. In the first days, you can soften early-morning light — keep things dim or wear
sunglasses outside — until your clock has shifted toward local time.

## Stay up to local bedtime

The goal is to delay sleep onset to match the destination. Evening light, light activity, and
keeping busy help you avoid an early crash and reach local bedtime, which is the fastest way
to settle a westward shift.
