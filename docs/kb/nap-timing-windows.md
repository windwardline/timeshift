# Nap Timing Windows

When in the day a nap helps rather than hurts.

## Aim for early afternoon

The best window for a recovery nap is usually early in the local afternoon, which lines up
with the body's natural mid-day dip. A short nap then refreshes you without stealing from the
coming night.

## Avoid the late-day nap

Napping too close to local bedtime steals sleep pressure from the night and pushes sleep
onset later — the opposite of what most travelers want. Keep naps well before evening, and
keep them short.

## The eight-hour guideline

A useful rule is to nap only if it is at least eight hours before your planned bedtime, and to
cap it at around half an hour. Outside that window, you are usually better off staying awake
and letting sleep pressure build for the night.
