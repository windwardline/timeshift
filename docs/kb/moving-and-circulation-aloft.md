# Moving and Circulation Aloft

Why getting up on a long flight is about more than comfort.

## Sitting still pools blood

Sitting for hours without moving lets blood pool in the veins of your legs, which on long
flights can raise the risk of clots. Long-haul travel is exactly when this matters, so
movement is a health measure, not just a way to stretch.

## Simple in-seat habits

Get up and walk the aisle periodically, and when seated, flex your calves and ankles to keep
blood moving. Avoid very tight clothing, and use the chance to rehydrate while you are up — all
of which also help you feel less stiff and groggy on arrival.

## Know your risk

Most travelers are at low risk, but certain conditions raise it. If you have specific risk
factors for clots, ask a clinician about precautions before a long flight rather than relying
on movement alone.
