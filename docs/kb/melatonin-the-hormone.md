# Melatonin, the Hormone

What your body's own melatonin does, before considering it as a supplement.

## The darkness signal

Melatonin is the hormone your body releases as darkness falls to signal that night is coming.
Its rise in the evening helps usher in sleep, and its timing is one of the clearest markers
of where your internal clock currently sits. It is a signal of biological night, not a
sedative that forces sleep.

## How light suppresses it

Bright light, especially blue-rich light from screens and LED bulbs, suppresses melatonin
release. Evening light can therefore push your melatonin later and delay your clock, while
darkness in the evening lets it rise on time. This is why managing evening light matters as
much as seeking morning light.

## Why travelers care

Because melatonin's timing reflects the clock, jetlag throws it off — your body may release
it during the destination day. Understanding the natural hormone makes the timing logic of
melatonin supplements, covered separately, much clearer.
