# How Fast the Clock Shifts

A planning rule for how quickly your body can adapt.

## About an hour a day

Left to itself, the body clock can only move by roughly one hour per day. So crossing six
time zones means on the order of several days to fully resynchronize if you do nothing to
help. This is the baseline that every adjustment strategy tries to beat.

## Speeding it up

Well-timed light, meals, sleep, and sometimes melatonin can push the clock faster than its
natural pace and in the right direction. The gains are modest per day but they add up, and
the difference between a planned approach and a haphazard one can be several days of
recovery.

## Setting expectations

Even with a good plan, a large eastward shift will not vanish overnight — the clock moves
gradually. Planning around partial adaptation, rather than expecting to feel perfect on
arrival, leads to better decisions about sleep, light, and important commitments.
