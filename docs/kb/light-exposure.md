# Light Exposure

Light is the most powerful tool for moving your body clock, if you time it right.

## Light is the master cue

Of all the signals that set the body clock, light is by far the strongest. Bright light in
the morning tends to shift your clock earlier, while bright light in the evening shifts it
later. Because light is so dominant, getting its timing right matters more than any
supplement or trick.

## Seek and avoid light by direction

Heading east, you generally want morning light at your destination and to avoid bright
light late in the evening, which helps pull your clock earlier. Heading west, you do the
reverse: seek light in the late afternoon and evening and go easy on very early morning
light. Matching light to your direction is the core of fast adjustment.

## Morning versus evening timing

The clock responds to light differently depending on when it lands. Light in the hours
after your body's natural wake time advances the clock, useful for eastward trips. Light in
the hours before your usual bedtime delays it, useful for westward trips. Mistiming light —
bright evening light when you are trying to go east — can actively push your clock the
wrong way.

## The role of sunglasses

Sunglasses are a way to block light when you specifically want to avoid it. For example, on
an eastward trip you might wear them to dim early-morning light during a layover so you do
not delay your clock at the wrong moment. Used deliberately, blocking light is as much a
tool as seeking it.

## Using arrival-day daylight

On the day you land, getting outdoors in natural daylight at the right time is one of the
fastest ways to anchor to local time. Even an overcast sky outdoors is far brighter than
indoor lighting and gives the clock a strong cue. Plan a walk timed to your direction —
morning light going east, afternoon light going west.
