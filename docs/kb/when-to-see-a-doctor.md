# When to See a Doctor

Most jetlag passes on its own — but some situations warrant medical advice.

## Usually self-limiting

Jetlag is temporary and typically improves over several days as your clock catches up. For
most travelers, light timing, schedule, hydration, and patience are enough, and no medical
care is needed.

## Reasons to seek help

Consider talking to a clinician if symptoms are severe or persist well beyond the expected
recovery window, if jetlag repeatedly disrupts your work or health as a frequent traveler, or
before using sleep medications. Anyone with a significant health condition should get tailored
advice.

## Beyond jetlag

Some post-flight symptoms are not jetlag at all — for example, leg swelling and pain or
shortness of breath can signal a clot and need prompt attention. When something feels wrong
rather than just tired, seek care rather than waiting it out.
