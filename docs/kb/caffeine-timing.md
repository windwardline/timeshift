# Caffeine Timing

Using caffeine to stay awake without wrecking the coming night.

## Fighting the afternoon dip

Caffeine can help you stay awake until local bedtime, especially through the mid-afternoon
energy slump on arrival day. A cup in the morning or early afternoon keeps you going so you
do not fall into a crash nap. The key is using it deliberately to hold your schedule.

## Stop early enough

To protect night sleep, stop caffeine by the early afternoon on local time — generally six or
more hours before your target bedtime. Evening caffeine can also suppress melatonin and delay
your clock, which is the opposite of what an eastward traveler wants.

## A tool, not a fix

Caffeine masks sleepiness; it does not move your clock or repay sleep debt. Use it to bridge
the day on local time, then let correctly timed light, meals, and sleep do the actual work of
adjustment.
