# Arrival Day Plan

A playbook for the first 24 hours at your destination.

## Adopt local time immediately

The single most important arrival-day move is to start living on local time the moment you
land. Eat, sleep, and be active according to the destination clock, not your home clock,
even if it feels wrong at first. Committing fully to local time gives every clock in your
body the same consistent signal.

## Local meals and sleep times

Take your meals at the destination's normal mealtimes and aim to sleep at the local
bedtime, even if you are only a little tired or not tired at all. Eating and sleeping on
the new schedule are strong cues that reinforce the shift. Resist the urge to eat a full
meal at 3 a.m. local time just because your stomach is on home time.

## Strategic nap versus push through

If you land exhausted, you face a choice: take a short strategic nap or push through to
local bedtime. A brief nap of 20 to 30 minutes early in the day can restore enough
alertness without wrecking that night's sleep. Pushing straight through is often the
cleaner choice when you arrive in the morning or midday, because it lets sleep pressure
build for a solid first night.

## Get outdoor light

Spend time outdoors in natural daylight on arrival day, timed to your travel direction —
morning light after flying east, afternoon light after flying west. Outdoor light is far
brighter than indoor lighting and gives the body clock its strongest anchor to local time.
A walk outside doubles as a way to stay awake until local bedtime.

## Avoid the crash nap

The biggest arrival-day trap is the long "crash nap" — lying down for two or three hours
in the late afternoon. A nap that long bleeds off the sleep pressure you need for the
night and can leave you wide awake at 2 a.m., resetting your progress. If you must nap,
keep it short and early.
