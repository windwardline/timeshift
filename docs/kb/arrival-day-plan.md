# Arrival Day Plan

A playbook for the first 24 hours at your destination.

## Commit to local time

The most important arrival-day move is to live on local time the moment you land — eat,
sleep, and be active on the destination clock even if it feels wrong. A single consistent
schedule gives every clock in your body the same signal.

## Nap or push through

If you land exhausted, choose between a short strategic nap and pushing through to local
bedtime. A 20–30 minute nap early in the day restores alertness without wrecking the night;
pushing straight through is cleaner after a morning arrival because it builds sleep pressure
for a solid first night.

## Get outdoor light

Spend time outdoors in daylight, timed to your direction — morning light after flying east,
afternoon light after flying west. Outdoor light is far brighter than indoor lighting and is
the strongest anchor to local time, while a walk doubles as a way to stay awake.
