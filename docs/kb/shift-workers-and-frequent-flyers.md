# Shift Workers and Frequent Flyers

Repeated clock disruption, and how to limit its toll.

## Chronic misalignment

People who fly often or work rotating shifts face repeated circadian misalignment, which can
accumulate as ongoing sleep loss and fatigue. The body never fully settles before the next
disruption, so the goal shifts from perfect adaptation to limiting harm.

## Practical strategies

Protect sleep opportunities with a dark, quiet, cool room and consistent pre-sleep routines;
use light deliberately to anchor alertness; and use caffeine strategically early in a shift,
not late. Short naps before or during long duty periods can preserve alertness.

## Know the limits

Some schedules cannot be fully adapted to, and chronic sleep loss carries real health and
safety risks. Sound habits help, but persistent problems are worth raising with an
occupational-health or sleep professional.
