# Melatonin Cautions

What melatonin is not, and who should be careful with it.

## Not a cure-all

Melatonin does not erase sleep debt and is not a substitute for good light and sleep timing.
It is one optional tool inside a larger plan, and on its own it will not overcome a poorly
timed schedule or a night of lost sleep.

## Who should check first

Effects and interactions vary between people. Those who are pregnant or breastfeeding, take
blood thinners or other medications, or have a health condition or epilepsy should talk with
a clinician before using it. Melatonin may also linger longer in older adults, causing
daytime drowsiness.

## Use it sensibly

Short-term use for travel is generally considered low-risk for healthy adults, but long-term
safety is less established. Keep doses modest, avoid combining it with alcohol or sedatives,
and keep products away from children, who can be harmed by accidental ingestion.
