# Melatonin for Eastward Trips

Using melatonin to help advance your clock when flying east.

## Why it helps going east

Eastward travel needs a phase advance — an earlier clock — which is the harder direction. A
low evening dose of melatonin at the destination reinforces the "night is coming" signal
earlier than your body would on its own, helping pull bedtime earlier.

## When to take it

The dose is built around the new, earlier bedtime you are trying to reach, taken some hours
before that target time rather than at your old home-time bedtime. Pair it with morning light
the next day, which advances the clock through a different mechanism.

## Keep it modest

Low doses are generally preferred because the goal is a clock signal, not sedation. Melatonin
supports the shift but does not replace good light timing; treat it as one part of an eastward
plan that also includes morning light and an earlier sleep schedule.
