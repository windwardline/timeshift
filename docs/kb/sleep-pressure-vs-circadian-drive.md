# Sleep Pressure vs. Circadian Drive

The two forces that decide when you feel sleepy.

## Two systems at once

Sleepiness comes from two systems. Sleep pressure builds steadily the longer you stay awake,
like a rising debt. The circadian drive depends on the time on your body clock, promoting
alertness during your biological day and sleep during your biological night.

## Why jetlag feels strange

In jetlag the two systems disagree: you may have plenty of sleep pressure yet feel wired
because your circadian drive still says "day," or feel crushingly sleepy mid-afternoon when
your clock says "night." The conflict is what makes jetlag fatigue come in waves.

## Using them

Short naps release sleep pressure, so a well-timed brief nap eases daytime fatigue while
leaving enough pressure to sleep at night. Light and schedule move the circadian drive.
Managing both — not just one — is how you settle quickly.
