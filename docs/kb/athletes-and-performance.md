# Athletes and Performance

Why competitors take jetlag seriously, and how they plan around it.

## Performance dips with the clock

Physical and mental performance follow the body clock, peaking during the biological day and
sagging near its low point. An athlete competing at what their clock thinks is the middle of
the night is at a real disadvantage, so jetlag can decide outcomes.

## Arrive early and pre-adjust

A common approach is to arrive several days ahead — often roughly one day per time zone — and
to pre-shift sleep and light before departure so the clock is closer to local time by event
day. Training and key sessions are scheduled for hours when the body will be alert.

## Stack the fundamentals

Disciplined light timing, consistent local meal and sleep schedules, hydration, and smart
napping all support faster adjustment. For high-stakes travel, the same grounded tools simply
matter more.
