# Eastward vs. Westward Travel

Why the direction you fly changes how hard jetlag hits.

## Why eastward is harder

Flying east forces your clock to move earlier, a shift called a phase advance. You must fall
asleep and wake sooner than your body wants, and the human clock resists being pushed earlier
because its natural tendency runs slightly long. Most travelers find an eastward trip
noticeably more punishing than the same distance west.

## Why westward is easier

Flying west asks your clock to move later, a phase delay. Staying up longer and sleeping in
lines up with the clock's natural drift toward a longer day, so it feels closer to an
ordinary late night. Westward recovery is usually faster and gentler for the same zones.

## East is early, west is late

A simple rule captures the strategy: "east = early, west = late." Heading east, shift
everything earlier — sleep, light, and meals. Heading west, push everything later. This one
rule drives most of the light and sleep timing advice.
