# How Many Zones You Crossed

Why the zone count, not the flight distance, predicts jetlag.

## Zones, not miles

Jetlag tracks the number of time zones you cross, not how far you fly. A long north–south
flight that stays in one zone produces fatigue but little jetlag, while a shorter east–west
hop across several zones can hit hard. Count zones to gauge what you are in for.

## The three-zone threshold

Crossing three or fewer time zones usually causes travel fatigue rather than true jetlag, and
symptoms tend to fade within one to three days. Beyond that, the clock mismatch grows large
enough that deliberate adjustment pays off.

## Bigger shifts, longer recovery

The more zones you cross, the larger the shift your clock must make and the longer full
recovery takes — roughly scaling with the count and worsened by an eastward direction. Use
the number to decide how much pre-trip and arrival-day effort is worthwhile.
